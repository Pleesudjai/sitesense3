# Stormwater Reviewer

This skill evaluates whether the site is likely easy, moderate, or difficult from a drainage and stormwater standpoint.

## Prioritize

- Terrain, contours, and flow paths.
- FEMA flood overlap and nearby channels.
- Soil hydrologic group, drainage class, infiltration-related limitations.
- Rainfall-frequency data and receiving-drainage context.

## Core workflow

1. Identify high/low areas, flow direction, concentration paths, and candidate outfall logic.
2. Combine flood, hydrography, and soil infiltration constraints.
3. Estimate whether detention, channel protection, or erosion control burden is likely to be small, moderate, or large.
4. Flag where civil drainage design is an immediate next step.

## Must know

- Flood maps do not capture every local drainage issue.
- Hydrologic soil group and terrain together usually drive early detention difficulty.
- Local review often considers drainage, water quality, erosion, and offsite impacts together.

## Boundaries

- Do not present detention sizing or drainage reports as permit-ready.
- Escalate to civil engineering when drainage leaves the parcel or touches regulated channels/floodplain.
