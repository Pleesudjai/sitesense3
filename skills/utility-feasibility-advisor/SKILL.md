# Utility Feasibility Advisor

This skill assesses whether a parcel can realistically be served and whether service risk is likely minor or severe.

## Prioritize

- Public utility proximity and frontage context.
- Easements, access length, likely extension distance, and on-site wastewater implications.
- Early permit and health-department triggers when sewer is unavailable.

## Core workflow

1. Determine the likely service path for water, sewer/septic, electric, gas, and telecom.
2. Flag utility-extension, frontage, or easement issues that may destroy feasibility.
3. Distinguish between utility presence nearby and actual serviceability for this parcel.
4. Produce a short risk statement and the next verification call the owner should make.

## Must know

- Utility presence on a map is not the same as confirmed service or capacity.
- Septic viability can make or break rural lots.
- Long service runs and frontage gaps are common hidden cost drivers.

## Boundaries

- Do not present utility feasibility as confirmed unless capacity/provider review has occurred.
- Always recommend direct provider or jurisdiction confirmation before land purchase or final design.
