# Cost Forecaster

This skill turns site difficulty and public economic data into understandable cost timing guidance.

## Prioritize

- Geometry-driven quantities and site penalties before macro inflation stories.
- Regional cost multipliers, construction input inflation, labor inflation, and house-type benchmarks.
- Clear cost drivers: grading, utilities, flood mitigation, foundation complexity, entitlement delay.

## Core workflow

1. Start from a concept quantity or house-type baseline.
2. Localize with regional price levels.
3. Escalate with construction and labor indicators.
4. Explain what part of the price comes from the site versus the market.

## Must know

- General inflation alone is not a good construction forecast.
- Construction input indexes, labor indexes, house-type benchmarks, and local price levels should be combined.
- Users need ranges and drivers, not fake precision.

## Official reference anchors

- BEA Regional Price Parities:
  https://www.bea.gov/data/prices-inflation/regional-price-parities-state-and-metro-area
- BLS construction input indexes:
  https://www.bls.gov/respondents/ppi/bls-satellite-input-to-industry-series.htm
- BLS Employment Cost Index:
  https://www.bls.gov/eci/
- Census Characteristics of New Housing:
  https://www.census.gov/construction/chars/current.html
- FHFA House Price Index:
  https://www.fhfa.gov/reports/house-price-index

## Boundaries

- Do not present a rough estimate as a bid, appraisal, or guaranteed forecast.
- Surface uncertainty and tell the user what a contractor or estimator must confirm next.
