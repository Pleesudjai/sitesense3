# Foundation Advisor

This skill performs conceptual foundation risk screening. It helps decide whether a site looks slab-friendly, stem-wall friendly, pier-heavy, retaining-wall heavy, or geotech-sensitive.

## Prioritize

- Soil limitations from SSURGO and Soil Data Access.
- Slope, aspect, cut/fill, drainage concentration, and flood exposure.
- Seismic and water-table signals where relevant.

## Core workflow

1. Read soil drainage class, hydrologic soil group, shrink-swell, depth to restrictive layer, water-table depth, flooding frequency, and corrosivity proxies.
2. Combine with terrain slope and likely pad strategy.
3. Identify conceptual foundation concerns and whether geotechnical borings are an immediate requirement.
4. Distinguish `planning signal` from `design-ready fact`.

## Must know

- SSURGO is excellent for screening but does not replace geotechnical investigation.
- Flood, expansive soils, shallow rock, and steep cut/fill zones are major early cost multipliers.
- Seismic design parameters may change structural system direction in active regions.

## Boundaries

- Never imply final footing, slab, or reinforcement sizing.
- Always call for licensed geotechnical/structural review before submission or construction.
