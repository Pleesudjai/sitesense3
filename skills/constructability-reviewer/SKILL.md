# Constructability Reviewer

This skill looks at whether the project is merely possible or actually practical to build.

## Prioritize

- Driveway/access geometry, frontage, slopes, retaining likelihood, staging area, and utility trenching difficulty.
- Site conditions that increase equipment difficulty, mobilization cost, or sequencing problems.
- Whether the concept creates unnecessary field complexity.

## Core workflow

1. Assess how crews, equipment, and materials would realistically reach and operate on the site.
2. Flag likely trouble points such as steep access, long service runs, excessive retaining, or narrow workable pads.
3. Translate those issues into schedule and cost implications.
4. Recommend simplifications when possible.

## Must know

- A buildable concept can still be a poor construction strategy.
- Access and earthwork often dominate early pain on difficult sites.
- Constructability comments should be operational and concrete, not vague.
