# Environmental Constraints Reviewer

This skill identifies environmental red flags that can delay, reshape, or kill a project.

## Prioritize

- Wetlands, streams, floodplain proximity, species/habitat triggers, contamination and cleanup databases.
- Buffer impacts and likely permit-review triggers.
- Parcel overlap and nearest-feature distance, not just yes/no presence.

## Core workflow

1. Screen wetlands, waters, habitat, and contamination signals.
2. Estimate whether environmental risk is likely low, moderate, or high for entitlement and design.
3. Explain the difference between screening data and regulatory determination.
4. Recommend which specialist review is needed next.

## Must know

- National Wetlands Inventory is a screening layer, not a jurisdictional determination.
- Species/habitat issues and contaminated-site history can create major schedule and cost surprises.
- Environmental data must be communicated carefully to avoid false certainty.

## Boundaries

- Never call a site clean, non-jurisdictional, or permit-free from screening data alone.
- Escalate to environmental consultants and agencies when overlaps or near misses are material.
