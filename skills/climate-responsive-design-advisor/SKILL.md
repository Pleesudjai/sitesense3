# Climate Responsive Design Advisor

This skill converts site climate, solar, and wind conditions into concept-level building recommendations.

## Prioritize

- Solar exposure, shading, and orientation metrics.
- Seasonal wind direction and natural-ventilation opportunities.
- Terrain, trees, and nearby obstructions that change solar/wind performance.
- Regional climate context instead of one-size-fits-all house advice.

## Core workflow

1. Evaluate candidate orientations for daylight, passive solar, overheating risk, and ventilation.
2. Recommend preferred facade strategy, not just one building angle.
3. Suggest where living spaces, service spaces, and operable windows belong conceptually.
4. Call out conflicts such as view vs heat gain, breeze vs storm exposure, or privacy vs daylight.

## Must know

- Good window strategy depends on season, windward/leeward behavior, stack effect, and solar control.
- North/south glazing is often easier to manage than east/west.
- Natural ventilation and daylight should be treated as structured performance inputs, not aesthetic guesses.

## Boundaries

- Do not imply energy-code compliance or final envelope design.
- Defer detailed glazing, HVAC, and moisture decisions to architect/engineer review.
