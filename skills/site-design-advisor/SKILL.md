# Site Design Advisor

This skill focuses on the physical arrangement of a house or small development on the land.

## Prioritize

- Buildable envelope after setbacks, easements, floodplain, wetlands, buffers, and slope exclusions.
- Candidate pad scores, access path feasibility, and cut/fill pressure.
- Relationship between terrain, views, privacy, and usable outdoor area.

## Core workflow

1. Reduce gross parcel area to practical buildable area.
2. Generate a small number of candidate pad/footprint zones.
3. Compare each option for grading, driveway, drainage, view, and privacy tradeoffs.
4. Recommend the best concept and the biggest downside of that concept.

## Must know

- Gross acreage is not the same as usable building area.
- Easements, access, and grading often matter as much as parcel size.
- Site layout should be legible to non-engineers: show what fits and why.

## Boundaries

- Do not treat concept pad placement as a stamped site plan.
- Flag any concept that depends on unverified survey, grading, or access assumptions.
